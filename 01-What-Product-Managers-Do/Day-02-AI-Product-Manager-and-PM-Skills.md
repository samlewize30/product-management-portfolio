# Day 2 – AI Product Manager & Product Manager Skills

## What I Learned

### Introduction to the AI Product Manager

- AI Product Managers combine traditional product management with AI knowledge.
- The demand for AI Product Managers is increasing because businesses are adopting Artificial Intelligence across industries.
- AI products require managing data, machine learning models, and continuous improvement.

### AI Product Manager vs Traditional Product Manager

| Traditional PM | AI Product Manager |
|---------------|--------------------|
| Focuses on customer needs | Focuses on customer and data needs |
| Works with software engineers | Works with engineers and data scientists |
| Uses fixed product roadmaps | Uses adaptive roadmaps based on AI model learning |
| Delivers software features | Delivers AI-powered, data-driven features |

### Essential Skills of an AI Product Manager

- AI literacy
- Data strategy
- Responsible and ethical AI
- Cross-functional collaboration
- Experimentation mindset
- Continuous learning

### Core Responsibilities of a Product Manager

- Understand customer needs.
- Define product vision and strategy.
- Create product roadmaps.
- Prioritize features.
- Collaborate with Engineering, Design, Marketing, and Sales.
- Represent the voice of the customer.
- Ensure products solve real problems.

### Key Product Management Skills

- User Experience (UX)
- Business Acumen
- Analytical and Strategic Thinking
- Communication
- Market Research
- Project Management
- Leadership
- Negotiation
- Time Management
- Problem Solving

### Business Acumen

Business acumen is the ability to understand business situations and make decisions that create value for both customers and the organization.

Important areas include:

- Financial literacy
- Strategic planning
- Market research
- Analytical thinking
- Leadership
- Communication
- Adaptability

### Financial Metrics Every Product Manager Should Understand

- Cash Flow
- Profit
- Return on Investment (ROI)
- Internal Rate of Return (IRR)
- Net Present Value (NPV)
- Payback Period

## Key Takeaways

- Product Managers act as the bridge between customers, business, and technical teams.
- AI Product Managers require both product management and AI knowledge.
- Strong communication and business thinking are as important as technical skills.
- Every product decision should create value for customers and the business.

## Personal Reflection

Today's lessons showed me that Product Management is much broader than managing software projects. It combines customer empathy, business strategy, data analysis, communication, and leadership. I also learned that AI Product Management is becoming one of the fastest-growing specializations and aligns well with my goal of building products in healthcare and AI.
